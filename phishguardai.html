What is Phishing?

Phishing is a social engineering attack mechanism where adversaries impersonate reliable entities to deceive targets into providing credentials, executing malicious attachments, or authorizing wire payments.

Common Threat Indicators

  • Lookalike Domains (Typosquatting): Domains mimicking trusted entities but containing subtle spelling variations (e.g., barclays-security-update.com instead of barclays.co.uk).
  • Coerced Urgency or Panic: Pressure statements demanding immediate actions within specific timeframes to bypass rational validation checks.
  • Credential Collection Links: Links routing to unauthenticated credential collection forms, external storage accounts, or unofficial cloud apps (like .xyz, .online, or .net).
  • Generic Sign-offs: Lack of specific recipient identification tags or using generic designations like "Valued Client".

Verification Checklist

Check the sender's actual root domain, not just their display name. Verify link targets by hovering or inspecting the raw URL in the browser's status bar before interacting.
